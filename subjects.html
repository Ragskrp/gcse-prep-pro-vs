Mathematics

Grade 7 Target

Subject Overview

Mathematics at GCSE level builds on your existing knowledge and introduces more advanced concepts. The course is divided into six main areas: Number, Algebra, Ratio and Proportion, Geometry and Measures, Probability, and Statistics. You'll develop problem-solving skills and learn to apply mathematical concepts to real-world situations.

The examination consists of three papers: one non-calculator and two calculator papers, each lasting 1 hour 30 minutes. Success in mathematics requires consistent practice, understanding of fundamental concepts, and the ability to show clear working in your solutions.

Computer Science

Grade 6 Target

Subject Overview

Computer Science GCSE introduces you to the fundamental principles of computing, programming, and computational thinking. You'll learn to write programs in Python, understand how computers work, explore networks and cybersecurity, and develop problem-solving skills.

The course combines practical programming with theoretical knowledge, preparing you for further study in computer science, software engineering, or related fields. Assessment includes practical programming tasks and written examinations.

French

Grade 6 Target

Subject Overview

French GCSE develops your ability to communicate effectively in French, both in speaking and writing. You'll learn essential grammar, vocabulary, and cultural knowledge while developing skills in listening, speaking, reading, and writing.

The course covers themes such as identity and culture, local, national, international and global areas of interest, and current and future study and employment. Assessment includes exams in all four skills plus a speaking endorsement.
